Privacy Policy

Last updated: November 6, 2025

Overview

Recovery Companion (“the App”) helps people track sobriety, discover meetings, and engage with supportive tools. We are committed to protecting your privacy and being transparent about how we handle your data.

Data We Collect

  • Account Information: Email address and password (hashed) when you create an account.
  • Profile Details (optional): Display name, avatar image, and basic preferences you choose to provide.
  • App Content: Sobriety dates and milestones, journal entries, favorites, reactions, and forum posts you create.
  • Device & Usage: Basic analytics such as device model, OS version, app version, and interactions to improve performance and reliability.
  • Location (optional): If you grant location permission, we use it to help find nearby meetings. You can revoke permission at any time in your device settings.

Sources of Meeting Data

The App aggregates meeting information from public and partner sources (e.g., AA/TSML, NA/BMLT, and other organizations). Meeting records may include venue names, addresses, schedules, formats, and publicly listed links (e.g., Zoom). If any listing is inaccurate, please email us and we will review it promptly.

How We Use Data

  • Provide core features: sobriety tracking, meeting discovery, journaling, and community interactions.
  • Maintain safety, prevent abuse, and comply with applicable laws.
  • Improve the App’s functionality and user experience.
  • Communicate service updates and important notices.

Data Sharing

  • No sale of personal data. We do not sell your personal information.
  • Service Providers: We use trusted vendors to host and operate the App (e.g., database, file storage, and analytics). Vendors only process data on our instructions and under confidentiality obligations.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose information where required by law or to protect the rights, safety, and security of users and the public.

Data Storage & Security

Your account data and app content are stored with reputable cloud providers and protected using industry-standard security practices. While no system is perfectly secure, we implement safeguards to reduce risk, including encryption in transit, access controls, and monitoring.

Retention

We retain personal data for as long as necessary to provide the App and comply with legal obligations. You can request deletion of your account and associated data at any time (see “Your Rights”).

Your Rights

  • Access & Correction: You can view and update profile information in the App.
  • Deletion: You can request deletion of your account and personal data.
  • Portability: You may request a copy of your personal data in a portable format.
  • Consent Controls: You can revoke location permissions and adjust notification settings at any time.

Children

The App is intended for users aged 13+ (or older where required by local law). We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under the minimum applicable age.

International Transfers

Data may be processed in the United States or other countries where our service providers operate. We take steps to ensure appropriate protections consistent with applicable law.

Changes to this Policy

We may update this policy to reflect product, legal, or operational changes. The “Last updated” date will indicate the latest revision.

Community Guidelines

To keep the community safe and recovery-focused, users must follow the rules below. Violations may result in content removal, account suspension, or termination.

Respect & Safety

  • No bullying, harassment, threats, or intimidation.
  • No hate speech or discrimination based on any protected characteristic.
  • No glorification of self-harm, violence, or illegal activity.

Recovery-Focused Content

  • No promotion or solicitation of substances or paraphernalia.
  • No buying, selling, or trading of controlled or prescription substances.
  • Advice shared should be supportive and peer-to-peer; the Service is not a substitute for professional care.

Privacy & Confidentiality

  • Do not share someone else’s private information (doxxing), including addresses, phone numbers, or other identifying data.
  • Do not post content you do not have the right to share (copyrighted material, confidential information).

Integrity of the Service

  • No spam, scams, or deceptive behavior.
  • No attempts to bypass safety systems, rate limits, or access controls.
  • Report violations through in-app tools where available or contact matthewschardt@schardtindustries.com.
