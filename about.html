About Me

I’m Matthew Schardt, a project manager in the energy sector and a current student at Carnegie Mellon University, pursuing advanced coursework in software development and systems design. My background in managing large-scale technical projects has built my foundation in coordination, process optimization, and cross-disciplinary collaboration — skills I now apply directly to building software that empowers people.

Outside of work, I’m developing Recovery Companion, a mobile application I’m building independently as a full-stack project. The app supports people in addiction recovery through journaling tools, milestone tracking, and community-driven support features. It’s built with React Native (Expo) on the frontend, Supabase for authentication and database services, and PostgreSQL for secure, scalable data management — all designed, coded, and deployed by me.

I’m passionate about merging structured project management with creative problem-solving in technology. My long-term goal is to bridge my experience in energy and operations with software that makes systems — and lives — run more efficiently
